Description
The aim of the UCSC computational economics reading group is to teach students how to compute equilibria in economic models that cannot be solved analytically. In the Fall quarter, the reading group will focus on the basics of computation: solving nonlinear equations, optimization, integration and function approximation. In the Winter quarter we will use these numerical tools to solve dynamic models, including standard macroeconomic models.
Course Materials
The primary textbooks for the reading group are Numerical Methods in Economics, Applied Computational Economics and Finance, and Dynamic General Equilibrium Modeling. The first book is the most complete reference, the second provides a softer introduction to most topics and the third contains a number of nice macroeconomic examples. Lecture slides will be prepared and presented by students and will be made available on this site.
Participation
Attendance is mandatory. In addition, students must participate in a lottery to determine the student presenters each week. Students are expected to present assigned materials each week, with illustrating examples, as well as to ask questions and participate in discussions during the workshop. Instructors will be present to help answer questions rather than to lecture.
